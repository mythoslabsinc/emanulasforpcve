The purpose of this resource is to provide users with the necessary knowledge and tools to effectively utilize social media to prevent and counter violent extremism (P/CVE).

This e-manual is structured step-by-step, guiding you through designing, implementing, and evaluating social media campaigns tailored to address specific P/CVE goals.

The E-Manual begins with an introduction to the significance of social media in P/CVE, and presents two guiding principles: "Do No Harm" and "Challenge not Change."

The following sections outline the steps to develop a successful P/CVE campaign:

Concept development

Determine the campaign goal, identify the target audience, select the most appropriate platform, and create a campaign concept.

Messaging

Choose the campaign style, select appropriate messengers, ensure their safety, and design the campaign's substance.

Dissemination

Develop a comprehensive dissemination approach to reach the target audience effectively.

Community building

Foster an online community that supports the campaign and encourages positive engagement.

Multiple-choice questions and activities are provided throughout each section to help you apply the concepts to your campaigns.

This e-manual also concludes with a list of resources, including a glossary, case studies, social media platform descriptions, and helpful references to assist practitioners in their P/CVE efforts.

By following the guidance and recommendations provided in this e-manual, community practitioners will be better equipped to create impactful social media campaigns that challenge extremist narratives and promote peace and tolerance.
